Braile Coding

Participants: Single person

About :

A simple coding fest with certain twists and turns which makes us feel the code and not just type the code. You need to solve given problems on the computer with your preferred language but the catch is, the screen will be turned off.

Rules & Regulations :

  1. Participants will have to solve the given problem within a defined timeframe.
  1. Points will be credited on basis of minimum errors.
  1. If there is a tie, the participants will have to code out a problem in a reverse manner i.e start from the end and finish at the beginning of the program.
  1. The qualifiers from Day 1 move on to the next round on Day 2.

Judgement Criteria :

  1. Judgement will be based on the logic, complexity and accuracy of the program.

Turncoat

Participants: Single Person

About:

Human beings are the most conflicting creatures on this planet. This event is an elucidation of this enthralling feature of ours. The participant will be given a topic to speak the pros and cons of the topic.

Rules & Regulations :

  1. It is a single person game.
  1. After getting the topic, the participant will be allotted 30 minutes for preparation. They are allowed to use any means such as the internet.
  1. After 30 min, the participant will have to speak on the given topic.
  1. They will be allotted 10 minutes time to speak on given topic. A cumulative 30 seconds will be allowed for failures and pauses in the entire span of 10 minutes.
  1. The participant has to switch the context of the topic as soon as the bell rings. If he or she is speaking on the Pros of the topic, he or she has to switch to the cons and vice versa.
  1. These are the pertinent rules and will be followed each day and eventually the best speaker wins.

Judgement Criteria:

  1. Judgement will be based on articulation, vocabulary, delivery, understanding & content of the topic.
  1. Posture and body language will also be an important factor towards winning.
  1. The final judgement lies solely in the hands of the coordinators.

Day 2:

Similar procedure will be followed as Day 1 but with the conglomeration of new inclusion of games with increased complexity such as finding the culprit from short video clips.

Rules & Regulations :

3 Musketeers

Date and Time: Day 1, 10AM

About

"All for One and One for All, united we stand, divided we fall" -Alexandre Dumas

This event exploits the theme behind The Three Musketeers to the core. Every human has their own pros but also have cons. When another person makes up for those cons, their teamwork can be accomplish almost anything.

Day 1:

On Day 1, all three team members will be given separate tasks to do simultaneously, both technical and non technical.

Day 2:

Similar to Day 1, but those who did the technical task on the previous day have to do the non technical task on this day and vice versa.

Day 3:

Third day will be a programming round where the qualifying teams will have to perform their task within given time limit.

Rules & Regulations

  1. The participant performing technical task in Round 1 cannot do the technical task in Round 2. He or she will have to the non technical task Round 2
  1. Time limit for all the tasks will be provided on the spot.
  1. Finalists of Day 3, the team member who still hasn't done any technical task will have to do the technical task.

Judging Criteria


  1. Judging and elimination will be fully based on the performance of the participants in the task.
  1. Eliminations will occur at the end of each day.
