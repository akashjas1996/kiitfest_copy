Hydrobooster

Description

Design a water rocket that is completely powered by the thrust delivered by water expelled at high pressure along with its Launchpad. Take your first big step to propel yourself into the world of aerodynamics! Water Rocket is a mechanical event that involves the fabrication of a rocket whose fuel/thrust for flight is obtained by the use of water that is expelled at a large air pressure. It is an example of Newton's third law of motion. The pre requisites for such an event would be a basic knowledge of fabrication, materials and physical calculations involving thrust, air pressure, etc. The event tests a couple of aspects of the Rocket’s flight. These include range, time of flight and accuracy of the launching mechanism.

Objective

First round: Range test

Here the maximum range is tested. The distance between the launch pad and the point of landing is measured and taken to be the points in that round. From this, top 5 will be shortlisted for the next round.

Second round: Accuracy test

At a distance of 70m from the launch pad, a target is placed. Target contains concentric circles of radii 2m, 4m and 6m. If the rocket lands at the centre, team gets 100 points, second ring 75 points, and outer ring 50 points. If otherwise, no points are awarded. Three teams will qualify for the final round. In the case of any tie, the time of flight will be noted and the team with the least time gets into lead.

Rules

  1. Max 5 participants. These Participants can be from different colleges too.
  2. The rocket and its components must be handmade. No readymade rocket is allowed.
  3. The rocket body should be made only with plastic bottles of aerated soft drinks of maximum capacity 2.5L and should be free of any fabrication failures or dents caused by heat exposure. More than one bottle can be used but the total volume must be less than 2.5L.
  4. Each team MUST have their own launch pads.
  5. The water rocket must use only compressed atmospheric air as its source of energy. Pressure compressors (foot pump) shall be provided at the venue. The pressure inside the container (rocket body) before launch should NOT exceed 60 psi for the first two rounds.
  6. Water to be filled in the rockets will be provided by the organizers. Calculations regarding amount of water to be used is left to the choice of the team.
  7. Two trials will be given to each team and best of two will be taken into consideration.
  8. The rocket must be launched from a stationary position using a fixed launch pad. Slingshots, trebuchets, catapults, cannons, and all other devices that may assist launching are strictly prohibited. IN other words, the internal pressure of the rocket must be the only source of energy for the rocket.
  9. Water rockets can have multiple stages. Parachute, gliding mechanisms are permitted.
  10. Use of electronic components and chemical explosives are banned.
  11. The point where the rocket hits the ground first will be taken as the point of landing and measurements will be taken considering this point.
  12. Teams are advised to get more than one water rocket, in case of any damage to one. But the water rockets must be identical.
  13. The participants are allowed to use variations of their water rockets in different rounds. However, only one design must be maintained throughout a single round.
  14. The team will be disqualified/given another chance to remodel if the model is found to be dangerous in anyway. Decision of the organizers is final and binding.

Judging Criteria

The judgment is completely based on the marking scheme mentioned in the event format.
The organizers decide to permit the launching of the rocket after inspection of both the rocket and launch pad considering factors such as safety and permissible mechanisms mentioned in the rules. In such cases, the organizers’ decision is final and binding.

F.A.Qs

1. Can school students participate as well? Totally. No barriers at all. 2. What will be the pre-requisites? Building a water rocket is both fun and quite an easy task. Basic designing and fundamentals in aero modelling would be sufficient. 3. Will launch pads be provided? Not in any circumstance. The team has to deal with their launch pads in case if it gets damaged or broken. 4. Will any materials be provided on the spot? The organization only provides water, a measuring cup, a bucket and compressors (which will have an in-built pressure gage). Other materials will NOT be provided. However, you could buy items in the stationary shops in the campus.

Sunder Muster

Description

This event includes the disassembling as well as assembling of a vehicle followed by a race. Team work along with the presence of mind is tested of participants. This is a team event, maximum team size should not exceed 5 members.

Qualification round:

A small group quiz test will be conducted comprising of 20marks to shortlist top 20 teams from the registered students.

Competition round:

First each team will be given a vehicle along with specific tools required to disassemble it. Time is noted till the vehicle is completely disassembled then inspection  of vehicle is done . Then the team has to assemble the vehicle  as fast as possible  and the time is also noted  followed by inspection.

 

Race:

After the inspection  if the vehicle is checked and declared as perfect by the judge the race is conducted. Race includes the participation of 2 people from each team . In  this race the riders have to travel a certain distance where a weight will be added to the vehicle and move further to complete the race  and time will be noted

Result:

All the time records will be added up and the team with completion of the work in least time will be declared as winner.

Tech Mania

Description

For all the quiz enthusiasts, we are organizing Random Access, a technical quiz contest. The contest will involve questions related to all Engineering Field in general elimination round . 1. The competition is open for participation to all University's students & don't forget to bring University or College  'I-CARD' . 2. A team must consist of two members.

Rules

Elimination Round-1(General Round)

This round is based on MCQ test so you are requested to carry your pen. There is no negative marking in this test.

Elimination Round-2(General visual round)

One question will be asked to each group, if answered correctly 10 marks will be awarded. If not answered, question will be passed to next group and 5 marks will be awarded if they answered correctly.

Final round (Buzzer round)

Total 20 questions will be asked. Chance will be given to the team which presses the buzzer first. If they answer correctly 10 marks will be awarded, if not correct 5 marks will be deducted.
The duration for each question to answer will be 30sec.
The judge’s decision will be final.
