Bio-Mind

Description

A quiz on Biotechnology, Life Sciences, and Allied Areas

General Rules

  1. Only team entries allowed.

  2. A team shall consist of two persons.

  3. The decision of the quiz-master will be final and will not be subjected to any change.

  4. The participants shall not be allowed to use mobile or other electronic instruments for the duration of the quiz.

  5. The questions shall be in the form of objective, short answer type, multiple choice questions, true/false type, etc.

  6. Audience shall not give any hints or clues to the competitors.

  7. Replacement of any participant of a team is not allowed after registration.

  8. Teams selected for the final rounds will be allowed to give themselves an appropriate name related to the competition by which they may want to be known.

  9. Tie-Breakers will be conducted as and when required at the discretion of the Quizmaster.

  10. The final winners will be declared after calculating the grand total of marks earned in all three stage rounds.

Format

ELIMINATION ROUND: (in case more than 6 teams are present on the quiz day)

  • Each team is given a set of questions containing 25 multiple choice objective type questions.

  • Time limit: 15 minutes.

  • Each correct answer will hold one mark.

  • There will be no negative marking for wrong answers.

  • Only 6 Teams would be selected for STAGE ROUNDS.

  • In case of a tie between 2 or more teams, further 5 questions would be asked for final selection.

  • The selected teams will take part in the STAGE ROUNDS.


FINAL QUIZ: STAGE ROUNDS:

Round 1: A question will be asked to a team and if they are unable to answer it will be passed to the next team – (4 Rounds, 24 questions)

  • Each team would be asked 4 questions each. ( Objective questions with options)

  • 10 marks for each correct answer and 5 negative marks for wrong answer.

  • If a team cannot answer the question, they can pass the question &  then the question would be forwarded to the next team.

  • Each time gets 30 seconds to answer to the question.

  • If a question is passed to the next team, they will only get 15 seconds to answer the question and and will be awarded 5 marks for correctly answering.

  • Team discussion is allowed.


Round 2: Rapid Fire Round: Each team is asked 10 questions one after another in one minute (60 seconds) time:

  • 10 marks for each correct answer and negative 5 marks for each wrong answer.

  • Answering time is only 3 seconds.

  • Team discussion is allowed.

  • If a team cannot answer the question, they can say pass for the next question. The question will not be forwarded to the next team.


Round 3: Audio-visual round: Each team is shown clips and will have to answer questions accordingly (12 questions):

  • 10 marks for each correct answer and negative 5 marks for each wrong answer.

  • Each Team would be asked 2 audio-visual questions (1 slide in each round).

  • Answering time is only 30 seconds.

  • No passing to the next team. Audience can answer when asked by the Quizmaster.


 ** SURPRISE ROUND SHALL BE INVOKED IN CASE OF TIE IN THE FINAL ROUND

PixeloScope

Description

A photo (micro) graph is a technical document that can be of great significance to science or industry. A good photograph is also an image whose structure, colour, composition, and content is an object of beauty, open to several levels of comprehension and appreciation.

The subject matter is unrestricted and any type of light microscopy technique is acceptable, including phase contrast, polarized light, fluorescence, interference contrast, darkfield, confocal, deconvolution, and mixed techniques.

General Rules

  1. Individual submission. Each entrant may submit up to three images for judging.

  2. Photos at least 8" x 10" at 300 dpi.

  3. Photographed safely in the appropriate BioSafety Level environment for the used organism(s), as laid down by the American Society of Microbiology.

  4. New and original; not seen/posted on the ‘net’ before or previously published or displayed elsewhere.

  5. Digitally untouched (or minimally edited only to improve clarity of the photo). No other artificial enhancement or manipulation (e.g., false colouring) is permitted.

  6. ‘Macro’photography is not permitted.

  7. ALL PHOTOS MUST BE EMAILED BY THE DEADLINE (to be updated).

Judging Criteria

Entries will be judged on:

  1. Originality

  2. Informational Content

  3. Technical Proficiency

  4. Artistic and Visual Impact

BioHack

Description

A hackathon focusing on the sustainable solutions using biotechnology and applied life sciences. BioHack has four main themes:

  1. “Health and Sanitation for All”

  2. “Safe Food and Drinking Water”

  3. “Clean and Green Earth”

General Rules

  1. Only team entries are eligible.

  2. A team shall consist of max 4 persons.

  3. Teams must choose a name and submit on the first day.

  4. It is a three day event.

  5. Each team must select one theme by the deadline (to be updated).

  6. Teams will be given case study according to chosen theme on the first day.

  7. All teams must bring their own laptops for research purposes.

  8. Teams will discuss an innovative and viable solution that has biotechnology at its core. The students must present the solutions in the form of powerpoint presentation.

  9. The teams will be allowed to consult or seek guidance from mentors and judges on the second day of the event.

  10. Judges will be evaluating the progress of solutions throughout the second day.

  11. By the end of the second day all teams must submit their final presentations.

  12. Maximum 3 teams per theme will be selected to present and explain their solution to the audience and judges on the third day of the event.

  13. Teams must maintain decorum during the discussion time.

  14. The decision of the judges will be final and will not be subjected to any change.

Guidelines

SUBMISSIONS must be:

  1. In the form of Powerpoint Presentations.

  2. Each presentation must be max. 10 slides and can contain graphics, images, text.

  3. Must be submitted at the end on second day.

  4. Judges will evaluate second day entries and select 3 teams for final presentation from each theme.

  5. Names of selected team will be announced on the third day.

  6. Presentation time for each time is 5 minutes; there will be negative marking on exceeding the time limit.

  7. Judges may ask questions (2 minutes duration separately) after the presentation.

  8. All presentations must be emailed to us by deadline (to be announced).

  9. Refrain from using slide transitions, animations, texts that are distracting from the theme of the competition.

  10. Original work, not plagiarised from any other sources. If needed a single additional slide containing references may be added.

Judging Criteria

Winners will be chosen based on the following criteria:

  1. Creativity/Originality of ideas.

  2. Scientific accuracy.

  3. Clarity of thought.

  4. Proper use of slides.

  5. Concise, logical and sequential explanation.

  6. Presentation skills such as engaging the audience, capturing interest, proper articulation and body language, etc.

TheMartian

Description

Design a futuristic extra-terrestrial organism.

Premise

The fictional Victor Frankenstein once created a monster called Frankenstein, it however did not turn out very well. But modern science has come a long way today. Emerging areas of science such as Synthetic Biology, Genetic Engineering & Biotechnology can now turn fiction into reality, thereby allowing mankind to create their own organism - one which will definitely not turn out to be a brute monster!

The search for extraterrestrial life is undoubtedly one of the most attractive topics in science. With the growing understanding of environment on Mars, it is believed that in the future affordable space transportation and colonisation of Mars will be possible. Using creative thinking and your knowledge of science, ideate and design an hypothetical life form - an organism that will be capable of life suited for specific environmental conditions on the planet Mars.

General Rules

  1. Only team entries are eligible.

  2. A team shall consist of max 3 persons.

  3. The decision of the judges will be final and will not be subjected to any change.

  4. Maximum 10 teams will be allowed to present.

Guidelines

SUBMISSIONS must be:

  1. In the form of Powerpoint Presentations.

  2. Each presentation must be max. 10 slides, and can contain graphics, images, text.

  3. Presentation time for each time is 5 minutes, there will be negative marking on exceeding time limit.

  4. Judges may ask questions in a separate 2 minute duration after the presentation.

  5. All presentations must be emailed to us by deadline (to be announced).

  6. Only 10 teams will be allowed to give oral presentation, the names of selected teams will be announced on the day of the competition.

  7. Refrain from using slide transitions, animations, texts that are distracting from the theme of the competition.

  8. An original work, not plagiarised from any other sources. If needed a single additional slide containing references may be added.

Suggestions: The following content must be ideally covered in your slides and main ideas must be further explained in the oral presentation.

  1. Name of your organism - scientific name and common name

  2. According to the current standard classification system, how would your organism be classified?

  3. The ecosystem, niche, biome of your organism.

  4. Cellular organisation of your organism.

  5. How does your organism metabolise?

  6. What does your organism look like? Explain the physical features, anatomy etc.

  7. How does your organism move and sense its surroundings?

  8. Is it a part of the food chain, where does it lie (producer, decomposer, etc?)

  9. What genetic variations exist in your organism?

  10. Is the organism related to any existing organisms? Mention any closely related ancestors that it may evolve from.

Judging Criteria

Winners will be chosen by our judges based on the following criteria:

  1. Creativity/ Originality of ideas

  2. Scientific accuracy

  3. Clarity of thought

  4. Proper use of slides

  5. Concise, logical and sequential explanation

  6. Presentation skills such as engaging the audience, capturing interest, proper articulation and body language, etc.
